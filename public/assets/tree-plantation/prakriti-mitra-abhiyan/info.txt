A seed ball is a seed that has been wrapped in soil materials, usually a mixture of mud and compost, and then dried. Essentially, the seed is ‘pre-planted’ and can be sown by depositing the seed ball anywhere suitable for the species, keeping the seed safely until the proper germination window arises.
These balls are prepared and kept safe until monsoon arrives. In monsoon, these balls will be scattered in suitable places. Once rain starts, seeds will germinate and the entire area will convert into thick forest.
The idea is to convert barren lands into oxygen factories !!



Seed Balls Preparation – want to start it in your city? Here is how !
Gather in your localities, schools and colleges. Take the initiative…your small efforts can bring about great changes! Making seed balls gives nature the extra boost it needs to make things right. Let’s join hands to spread this technique and give mother nature a helping hand 🙂
Steps to make Seed Balls :
1. Take 2 parts of humus/compost. It provides nutrients and holds moisture.
2. Take 1 part of clay. Remove stones or other unwanted particles from it. Clay serves to bind the seed balls together.
3. Take ½ parts of hawan/yagya ash (if available). The ash contains potassium and magnesium which is beneficial for the seed to grow into a plant.
4. Mix the above mentioned ingredients.
5. Knead the mix with your hands by adding water so that the mixture is workable enough to hold the ball together, but it should not be sticky. If it gets sticky add compost until you get the right consistency. There should be no lumps.
6. The consistency of the mixture should be like that of a toy molding clay. The ball prepared, should not have cracks.
7. Now, roll the mixture into balls, 3-4 cm in diameter and with the use of a finger, make a deep hole in the ball so that later on seeds can be inserted into that hole. (Seeds are not inserted at this stage. They should be inserted during the monsoons before you want to throw the ball at suitable places for tree plantation. If inserted now, they will start germinating). 
8. Allow the seed balls to dry under the sun for 24-48 hours.
9. After it is dried, your seed ball is prepared.
10. Now, during monsoons insert 2-3 seeds(if the seeds are small like papaya else one is enough in case of mango etc.) into the seed ball, cover the hole with clay.
11. You may throw the seed balls wherever you want to plant trees like in barren areas, near lakes, in parks or even in countryside while travelling from one city to another and mother nature will take care of the rest

Gayatri Pariwar, Bangalore started seed ball preparation activity ahead of the monsoon season as part of "Prakruti Mitra Abhiyan". The initiative got its wings and is spreading in different states and cities of the country.
Last Sunday on 3rd June 2018 ,Gayatri Pariwar - Lucknow joined hands in this initiative and came together for a seed ball making session where people of all age groups and backgrounds actively participated in the activity and prepared more than 500 seed balls which will turn into oxygen factories in the future. They not only took part in the activity but also greatly appreciated this technique for tree plantation as they found it to be an easy, cost effective form of natural farming where no tillage and very less efforts are required to grow plants. People can start this activity in their homes and localities. For those people who can’t go and plant trees, this is a simple way to grow trees and save the environment.
